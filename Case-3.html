SOLAR ENERGY

Dec 7, 2023

DEFINATION

Solar energy is a renewable and clean source of energy that harnesses the power of the sun's radiation. Solar panels, also known as photovoltaic (PV) modules, are devices that convert sunlight directly into electricity through the photovoltaic effect.

Pros :

1. Renewable and Sustainable: Solar energy is a virtually inexhaustible and renewable source of energy, as long as the sun continues to shine.

2. Clean and Environmentally Friendly: Solar energy does not produce greenhouse gases or other pollutants during operation, making it an environmentally friendly energy source.

3. Low Maintenance Costs: Once installed, solar panels require minimal maintenance, resulting in low operating costs.

4. Distributed Generation: Solar panels can be installed on rooftops or in small-scale solar farms, allowing for distributed generation and reducing transmission losses.

Cons :

1. High Initial Costs: The upfront costs of installing solar panels can be significant, although the long-term savings on electricity bills can offset these costs over time.

2. Weather Dependence: Solar energy production is dependent on sunlight availability, which can be affected by factors such as cloud cover, seasonal variations, and geographic location.

3. Space Requirements: Large-scale solar projects can require significant land area, which may lead to land-use conflicts or environmental concerns.

4. Recycling and Disposal: Solar panels contain materials that need to be properly recycled or disposed of at the end of their lifespan to minimize environmental impact.

Electricity Generation from Solar Energy

Solar energy is primarily harnessed through two main technologies: photovoltaic (PV) and concentrating solar-thermal power (CSP) . Photovoltaic solar power involves converting solar energy into electricity using solar panels, while concentrating solar power uses thermal energy to generate electricity. Both technologies play a crucial role in harnessing the sun's energy for electricity production.

GOVERNMENT SCHEMES FOR SOLAR PANELS

Many governments offer various schemes and incentives to promote the adoption of solar panels. example, some states offer solar renewable energy certificates (SREC), which represent a megawatt-hour of electricity generated through solar energy.
Electricity suppliers buy these certificates to satisfy their states Renewable Portfolio Standard, encouraging the use of solar energy .
Additionally, governments often provide tax rebates, feed-in tariffs, and other financial incentives to make solar panel installation more affordable for consumers.
Energy continues to be a key player in the transition to clean and sustainable energy sources, with ongoing advancements and government support driving its widespread adoption.
